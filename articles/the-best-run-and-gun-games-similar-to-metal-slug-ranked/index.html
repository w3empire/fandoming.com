Top 30 Best Run and Gun Games Similar to Metal Slug Ranked

authorElliot "PixelProwler" Parker

If you’re a fan of high-octane action, relentless enemies, and a generous dose of adrenaline, then run and gun games are right up your alley. Inspired by the classic Metal Slug series, these games are a thrilling rollercoaster ride filled with bullets, explosions, and pixelated chaos.

In this list, we’ll take a deep dive into the top 30 best run and gun games that share the same heart-pounding spirit as Metal Slug.

Each game on this list offers a unique experience, from alien invasions to futuristic battles, and they all have one thing in common: unapologetic, action-packed fun.

So, grab your favorite controller and get ready to embark on a journey through the world of run and gun gaming.

30. Super Turrican (1993) - SNES

Super Turrican takes the 30th spot on our list, but don’t underestimate its prowess. Released for the Super Nintendo, this game throws you into a vibrant world filled with monstrous creatures and hordes of enemies.

The gameplay is fast-paced, with our hero armed to the teeth, shooting in all directions and even employing a handy grappling hook.

Playing Super Turrican feels like a blast from the past, and it’s a reminder of why the SNES was such a beloved console.

The game’s challenging levels and memorable boss fights keep you on your toes, and the controls are tight and responsive.

Super Turrican might not be as famous as some other titles on this list, but it’s a hidden gem that deserves a spot in the run and gun hall of fame.

29. Viewtiful Joe (2003) - GameCube, PS2

Viewtiful Joe, released for the GameCube and PS2, is a unique blend of run and gun and beat ’em up genres.

The game follows the adventures of Joe, a regular guy who gains superpowers and becomes a hero in a movie world.

Viewtiful Joe is a quirky and refreshing take on the run and gun genre. The cel-shaded graphics are a visual delight, and the gameplay is a thrilling mix of puzzle-solving and combat.

Joe’s abilities to slow down or speed up time add a layer of strategy to the battles, making it a standout title that deserves more recognition.

28. Atomic Runner Chelnov (1988) - Arcade, Sega Genesis

Atomic Runner Chelnov, originally an arcade game and later released on Sega Genesis, offers a unique twist on the run and gun formula.

You play as Chelnov, a coal miner turned superhero, battling enemies with a jetpack and a rapid-firing nuclear arm.

The game’s setting is a post-nuclear world filled with bizarre enemies, creating a surreal and captivating atmosphere.

Atomic Runner Chelnov is an underrated gem that blends science fiction and action seamlessly. The jetpack mechanic adds a vertical element to the gameplay, making it a standout among its peers.

While it may not have gained the same level of recognition as some other run and gun classics, it’s a title that deserves a revisit for its unique charm and challenging gameplay.

27. Wild Guns (1994) - SNES

Wild Guns takes us to the wild west, where you play as Clint or Annie, bounty hunters on a mission to take down outlaws and robotic enemies.

This SNES classic offers a blend of shooting and dodging mechanics, with a unique aiming system that adds depth to the gameplay.

The game also supports cooperative play, making it a great choice for multiplayer action.

Wild Guns is a hidden treasure that combines the rugged charm of the old west with futuristic weaponry. The game’s isometric perspective and quick reflexes keep you engaged, and the cooperative mode enhances the fun factor.

Wild Guns is proof that run and gun games can thrive in diverse settings, and it’s a must-play for fans of the genre.

26. Contra: Hard Corps (1994) - Sega Genesis

Contra: Hard Corps, unleashed on the Sega Genesis, is an epitome of hardcore run and gun action. It carries the legacy of the Contra series with its intense battles and brutal difficulty.

You’ll face a barrage of enemies, menacing bosses, and a wide array of weapons to keep you on your toes. This installment is notable for the inclusion of multiple playable characters, each with a unique set of abilities and weapons, adding layers of replayability.

Contra: Hard Corps is a game that demands dedication and perseverance. It’s tough as nails, and you’ll die—a lot.

But that’s part of the charm. The satisfaction that comes from conquering its challenges is unparalleled. The variety of characters and paths you can take in the game adds depth, making every playthrough feel fresh.

If you’re up for a punishing yet exhilarating run and gun experience, Contra: Hard Corps is your ticket to gaming bliss.

25. Ranger-X (1993) - Sega Genesis

Ranger-X for the Sega Genesis is a game that often flies under the radar, but it deserves a spot on this list for its unique take on the genre.

In Ranger-X, you pilot a mech suit with the ability to switch between a flying motorcycle and a bipedal robot. The gameplay is fast-paced, with a wide range of weapons and gadgets at your disposal.

It’s a mix of platforming, shooting, and strategy as you navigate hostile environments and take down colossal bosses.

Ranger-X is a hidden gem that showcases the Sega Genesis’ capabilities. The game’s graphics are impressive for its time, and the blend of on-foot and mech-based combat keeps the gameplay engaging.

It’s not as well-known as some other titles on this list, but if you’re a fan of mecha and run and gun action, Ranger-X is worth seeking out for a dose of 16-bit nostalgia.

24. Alien Soldier (1995) - Sega Genesis

Alien Soldier, another Sega Genesis entry, is a frenetic run and gun game that’s often overshadowed by its contemporaries.

You play as Epsilon-Eagle, a super-soldier with the power to morph into various forms, each with unique abilities.

The game boasts a staggering 25 boss battles and non-stop action that will test your reflexes and strategic thinking.

Alien Soldier is a testament to the creativity of game developers in the 16-bit era.

The ability to switch between forms adds a layer of strategy to the intense battles, and the boss fights are some of the most challenging you’ll encounter in any run and gun game.

23. Metal Warriors (1995) - SNES

Metal Warriors for the SNES is a game that oozes mecha mayhem. You control customizable battle mechs with various weapons and abilities, engaging in intense 2D battles.

The game’s focus on piloting these mechanical marvels in tight, action-packed levels sets it apart from traditional run and gun titles.

Metal Warriors captures the essence of piloting giant robots and infuses it with exhilarating action. The ability to eject from your mech and take control of others adds a strategic element to the gameplay.

It’s a unique twist on the run-and-gun genre and offers a refreshing change of pace. If you’ve ever dreamt of being a mech pilot, Metal Warriors is the game that lets you live out that fantasy.

22. Cybernator (1992) - SNES

Cybernator, also known as “Assault Suits Valken” in Japan, is a hidden gem for the SNES that combines mecha combat and run and gun action.

You step into the cockpit of a powerful assault suit, armed with an array of weapons and the ability to jet through the battlefield.

The game has impressive visuals for its time, with detailed backgrounds and mecha designs that still hold up today.

Cybernator is a captivating blend of intense combat and atmospheric storytelling. The sense of piloting a massive mech is palpable, and the game’s difficulty ramps up nicely, offering a satisfying challenge.

21. Duck Game (2014) - PC, PS4, Nintendo Switch

Duck Game is a quirky and chaotic run and gun game that takes a lighthearted approach to the genre.

You play as a customizable duck armed with an assortment of absurd weapons, competing against other ducks in fast-paced battles.

The game’s pixel art style and physics-based gameplay create hilarious and unpredictable moments.

Duck Game is a wild ride of feathered mayhem that’s perfect for multiplayer gaming sessions. The sheer randomness of the weapons and the zany physics make every match feel fresh and entertaining.

It’s a departure from the traditional run and gun formula but a delightful one that guarantees laughs and memorable moments.

20. Strider (1989) - Arcade, various platforms

Strider is a classic run and gun game that introduces players to Hiryu, a ninja-like warrior armed with a plasma sword.

This arcade sensation was later ported to various platforms, including the Sega Genesis and NES.

Strider’s fast-paced action and non-linear level design set it apart from other run and gun games of its era.

Strider is a timeless classic that combines ninja-style acrobatics with intense combat. The ability to climb walls and ceilings adds a unique dimension to the gameplay, and the game’s world design is both captivating and challenging.

Strider has left an indelible mark on the run and gun genre, and it’s a title that every gamer should experience.

19. Blackthorne (1994) - SNES, Sega 32X, PC

Blackthorne is a dark and atmospheric run and gun game that delves into the realm of fantasy.

You play as Kyle Vlaros, a warrior with a shotgun, navigating through shadowy landscapes filled with danger.

The game is known for its deliberate pace, environmental puzzles, and satisfying “cover and shoot” mechanic.

Blackthorne is a moody and captivating run and gun experience that stands out from the crowd. The game’s dark aesthetic and methodical gameplay make for a unique and engaging adventure.

18. Cave Story (2004) - PC, various platforms

Cave Story is a beloved indie game that marries the run and gun genre with a touching storyline and charming pixel art. You play as a character named Quote who awakens in a mysterious cave and becomes embroiled in a battle against evil creatures and a power-hungry scientist.

The game is known for its nonlinear progression, offering multiple story paths and endings.

Cave Story is a testament to the creativity and passion of indie game developers. Its heartwarming story, memorable characters, and challenging gameplay make it a standout in the genre.

The fact that it was largely created by one person, Daisuke “Pixel” Amaya, adds to its appeal. Cave Story is a true gem that has captured the hearts of gamers worldwide.

17. Bionic Commando (1987) - Arcade, NES, various platforms

Bionic Commando is a classic NES title that blends run and gun action with a unique grappling hook mechanic.

You control Rad Spencer, a soldier with a bionic arm that allows him to swing from platforms and attack enemies. The game is known for its challenging levels and memorable boss fights.

Bionic Commando is a nostalgia trip for gamers who grew up with the NES. The grappling hook mechanic adds depth to the gameplay and sets it apart from other run and gun titles.

It’s a game that rewards precision and strategy, making each victory feel hard-earned.

Yeah, you can say that the graphics may be dated by today’s standards, but the gameplay still holds up, making Bionic Commando a must-play for retro enthusiasts.

16. Gunforce II (1994) - Arcade

Gunforce II is an arcade run and gun game that delivers explosive action in classic 2D style. You take on the role of a soldier battling terrorists and enemies across various locations.

The game is known for its relentless pace, massive explosions, and a wide array of weapons.

Gunforce II is a true arcade gem that revels in over-the-top action. The game’s non-stop explosions and frenetic combat keep you engaged from start to finish.

It’s a throwback to the golden age of arcade gaming when quarters were precious, and high scores were a badge of honor.

If you have a soft spot for old-school run and gun action, Gunforce II is a title that should not be overlooked.

15. Jamestown (2011) - PC, PS4, Nintendo Switch

Jamestown is a game that takes you to an alternate history where British colonialists have settled on Mars.

You play as a brave pilot battling hordes of Martians in intense bullet-hell-style combat. The game supports cooperative play and offers a unique “Vaunt” mechanic that allows you to boost your abilities for a limited time.

Jamestown brings a fresh twist to the run and gun genre. The bullet-hell elements add a layer of challenge, and the cooperative mode enhances the fun factor.

The game’s alternate history setting and steampunk aesthetics create a visually stunning backdrop for the action. If you’re a fan of shoot ’em ups and run and gun games, Jamestown is a must-play that combines the best of both worlds.

14. Run Saber (1993) - SNES

Run Saber is a side-scrolling run and gun game for the SNES that often flies under the radar. In this game, you control one of two characters with unique abilities, armed with energy blades to slice through enemies.

The game’s fast-paced action, unique power-ups, and impressive boss battles set it apart.

I can safely say that Run Saber is an interesting game that fans of the genre should seek out. The ability to dash, jump, and slice through enemies with energy blades feels satisfyingly fluid.

13. Mercenary Kings (2014) - PC, PS4, Xbox One, Nintendo Switch

Mercenary Kings is a modern run-and-gun game that combines the classic pixel art style with deep customization and cooperative play.

You play as a member of an elite team of mercenaries tasked with taking down a nefarious organization. The game offers a wide range of weapons, gadgets, and armor to craft and customize, allowing for a personalized playstyle.

Mercenary Kings also provides players with a refreshing take on the run-and-gun genre with its emphasis on customization and cooperative play.

The game’s pixel art is both charming and detailed, and the variety of missions and objectives keep the gameplay engaging.

It’s a great title for playing with friends and strategizing your approach to missions. Mercenary Kings is a modern game that pays homage to the classics while adding its own unique flair.

12. Shock Troopers (1997) - Neo Geo, Arcade

Shock Troopers is a game that takes you on a military mission to rescue hostages and take down a powerful terrorist organization.

What sets Shock Troopers apart is its multiple playable characters, each with their own special abilities and playstyle.

The game offers both top-down and isometric perspectives, adding variety to the gameplay.

Shock Troopers is a Neo Geo classic that shines in the run-and-gun genre. The ability to choose from a roster of characters adds replayability, as each offers a distinct experience.

The game’s top-notch pixel art and intense action make it a standout title for fans of arcade-style run-and-gun games. It’s a must-play for anyone seeking a nostalgic yet thrilling gaming experience.

11. Broforce (2015) - PC, PS4, Nintendo Switch

This game pays homage to action movie heroes of the ’80s and ’90s. You control a roster of characters inspired by iconic action stars, each armed with a unique weapon.

The game’s destructible environments and cooperative multiplayer add chaos and laughter to the mix.

Broforce is an absolute blast to play, especially with friends. The game’s pixel art and humor create a lighthearted and exhilarating experience.

It’s a celebration of action movie clichés and a non-stop explosion fest that keeps you entertained throughout.

Broforce captures the essence of ’80s and ’90s action films and translates it into gaming brilliance.

10. Blazing Chrome (2019) - PC, PS4, Xbox One, Nintendo Switch

Blazing Chrome is a modern love letter to the classic run and gun games of yesteryear. It channels the spirit of titles like Contra and Metal Slug, offering intense side-scrolling action with a dash of nostalgia.

You choose between two characters, each with their own unique abilities, and blast your way through hordes of robotic enemies in a post-apocalyptic world.

Blazing Chrome is a glorious revival of the run and gun genre. It captures the essence of ’90s arcade classics with its pixel art, tight controls, and challenging gameplay.

The game’s explosive set pieces and epic boss battles make it a thrilling ride from start to finish. If you’re a fan of old-school run and gun action, Blazing Chrome is a must-play.

9. Hard Corps: Uprising (2011) - PS3, Xbox 360

Hard Corps: Uprising is a spiritual successor to the Contra series, and it lives up to its legacy. The game combines fast-paced action with modern aesthetics, offering a visually stunning run and gun experience.

You play as soldiers battling against a powerful empire, armed with an arsenal of weapons and power-ups.

The game’s gorgeous 2.5D graphics and anime-inspired art style make it stand out in the genre. The challenging levels and epic boss fights keep you on your toes, and the cooperative mode enhances the fun factor.

It’s a modern classic that pays homage to its roots while delivering a fresh and exhilarating gaming experience.

8. Contra (1987) - NES

Contra, the game that laid the foundation for the run and gun genre, needs no introduction. It’s the epitome of classic side-scrolling action, with iconic characters like Bill and Lance fighting against an alien invasion. The game is known for its intense difficulty and memorable “Konami Code.”

Contra is a legendary title that has left an indelible mark on the gaming world. Its challenging gameplay and adrenaline-pumping action set the standard for the entire genre.

The “spread gun” is a weapon of pure joy, and the game’s cooperative mode adds an extra layer of excitement. Contra is a timeless classic that continues to captivate gamers and stands as proof of the golden age of gaming.

7. Sunset Riders (1991) - Arcade, SNES, Sega Genesis

Sunset Riders is a wild west-themed run and gun game that puts you in the boots of bounty hunters out to capture notorious outlaws.

The game features fast-paced, two-player cooperative action with colorful characters and memorable boss fights. It’s known for its humor and iconic “Wanted” poster bosses.

Playing Sunset Riders is like a nostalgic trip to the arcade era, and it’s a blast to play with friends. The game’s western setting and humorous character design create a unique atmosphere.

It’s a prime example of cooperative run and gun gameplay done right, and it’s a title that has earned its place in the hearts of gamers who grew up with it.

6. Alien Hominid (2004) - PS2, GameCube, Xbox, PC

Alien Hominid has quirky sense of humor and unique art style. You control a cute yet deadly alien trying to escape from government agents.

The game features frenetic shooting, vehicle segments, and cooperative play.

Alien Hominid is a delightful and chaotic run and gun experience. Its hand-drawn art style and sense of humor set it apart from other titles in the genre.

The game’s cooperative mode adds to the mayhem, and the sheer variety of enemies and power-ups keeps the gameplay fresh.

5. Mega Man X (1993) - SNES

Mega Man X takes the fifth spot on our list and is an iconic title in the run and gun genre. It introduces players to X, a new generation of Mega Man, with enhanced abilities and armor upgrades.

The game offers non-linear level progression, allowing you to tackle stages in any order, and it’s known for its challenging boss battles and tight controls.

Mega Man X is a masterpiece of classic gaming. The game’s ability to mix platforming and shooting is nothing short of brilliant, and the excitement of defeating each Maverick boss and acquiring their powers is unparalleled.

The soundtrack is like chocolate to my ears, and the game’s replayability is off the charts. Mega Man X is a true classic that has stood the test of time and remains a must-play for any fan of run-and-gun action.

4. Cuphead (2017) - PC, Xbox One, PS4, Nintendo Switch

Cuphead is a visually stunning and challenging game inspired by 1930s cartoons. You play as Cuphead or Mugman, battling through a series of boss fights and platforming levels.

The game is renowned for its hand-drawn animation, unique art style, and unforgiving difficulty.

Cuphead is a work of art that pushes the boundaries of what run and gun games can be. Its painstakingly crafted visuals and jazzy soundtrack transport players to a bygone era of animation.

The boss fights are an adrenaline rush, and the sense of accomplishment after defeating each one is immense. Cuphead is a modern classic that combines nostalgia with innovation, delivering an unforgettable gaming experience.

3. Gunstar Heroes (1993) - Sega Genesis

Gunstar Heroes is a fast-paced and explosive run-and-gun game that ranks third on our list. You play as Red or Blue, twin heroes armed with a variety of weapon combinations, thanks to the “Weapon Force” system. The game features non-stop action, epic boss battles, and cooperative play.

Gunstar Heroes is an absolute masterpiece in the Sega Genesis library. The ability to mix and match weapon types adds depth to the gameplay, and the variety of levels keeps you engaged from start to finish.

The game’s boss battles are legendary, and the cooperative mode allows for epic gaming sessions with friends. Gunstar Heroes shows the power of the 16-bit era and is a must-play for any run-and-gun enthusiast.

2. Contra III: The Alien Wars (1992) - SNES

Contra III: The Alien Wars secures the second spot on our list, and it’s a game that needs no introduction. It’s a direct sequel to the original Contra and takes the series to new heights with its intense action and top-notch graphics.

Players control heroes battling against an alien invasion with an array of powerful weapons and memorable set pieces.

Contra III: The Alien Wars is a timeless classic that defined the run and gun genre. Its level of intensity and challenge is legendary, and the thrill of overcoming seemingly insurmountable odds is unforgettable.

The game’s “Mode 7” graphics were groundbreaking for the SNES, and the cooperative mode adds to the excitement. Contra III is a quintessential run-and-gun title that has left an indelible mark on the gaming world.

1. Demon Front (2002) - Arcade

And now, we’ve reached the number one spot on our list of the top 30 best run-and-gun games similar to Metal Slug: Demon Front. This arcade hidden gem is often overshadowed by its peers but deserves recognition as the pinnacle of run-and-gun action.

I would define Demon Front as a tour de force in run-and-gun gaming. Its stunning 2D visuals, massive bosses, and diverse gameplay mechanics make it a standout title in the genre.

The game’s cooperative mode enhances the fun factor, and the variety of playable characters adds replayability.

Demon Front may not have the same level of recognition as some other titles on this list, but it embodies the essence of what makes run-and-gun games so exhilarating.

It’s a really cool game that deserves a spot in the pantheon of classic arcade games.
