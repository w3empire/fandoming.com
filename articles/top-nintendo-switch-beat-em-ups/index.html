The Best Nintendo Switch Beat 'Em Ups (Our Top Picks)

authorElliot "PixelProwler" Parker

Are you a proud owner of a Nintendo Switch? And you’ve got a hankering for some good old beat ’em up action? Then you’re in for a treat. The Switch boasts a variety of beat ’em up titles that cater to different tastes and preferences, and today we’re going to dive deep into some of the best of the bunch.

But first, let’s talk about why beat ’em ups are so darn popular on the Switch. With its portable capabilities and diverse library, it’s no wonder gamers are flocking to the console.

Plus, there’s just something immensely satisfying about smashing baddies with your fists or a variety of weapons, right?

Fight’N Rage (2019)

Kicking things off, we have “Fight and Rage,” a title that’s sure to get your adrenaline pumping. This retro-inspired gem brings back memories of classic arcade beat ’em ups, and it’s an absolute blast to play.

Behind the scenes, this game was crafted with love by indie developer Seba Games Dev. The inspiration? Well, think Double Dragon and Final Fight with a modern twist.

What makes “Fight and Rage” stand out is its depth. It offers multiple playable characters, each with unique abilities, and a combo system that’ll have you chaining moves like a pro. Plus, the co-op mode is a riot with friends.

Visually, it’s a pixelated paradise, and the soundtrack delivers that old-school arcade vibe. It’s a must-play for anyone looking to relive the glory days of arcade beat ’em ups.

Jitsu Squad (2022)

Next up, we’ve got “Jitsu Squad.” This hidden gem is a pleasant surprise for those willing to explore lesser-known titles.

The gameplay is smooth as butter, and the characters are a quirky bunch. Each ninja has their unique skills, and the fast-paced combat keeps you on your toes.

With its vibrant visuals and catchy tunes, “Jitsu Squad” oozes charm. It’s a fantastic addition to your Switch library if you’re up for some ninja action.

Final Vendetta (2022)

Here’s one for the die-hard beat ’em up fans.

This game takes a more strategic approach to combat, with an emphasis on combos and counters. The character roster is diverse, catering to different playstyles.

Visually, it’s a stunner, with detailed character designs and gorgeous backgrounds. The soundtrack complements the action beautifully.

The Ninja Saviors: Return of the Warriors (2019)

Prepare to be blown away by “Ninja Saviors Return of the Warriors.” It’s a remake of a classic, and it’s a masterclass in old-school beat ’em up goodness.

You’ve got a handful of ninjas to choose from, each with their skills. The difficulty curve is just right, providing a challenge without being too frustrating.

This game looks and sounds incredible. The character sprites are top-notch, and the music is a nostalgic trip back to the ’90s arcade scene.

Capcom Belt Action Collection (2022)

Capcom fans, rejoice! The “Capcom Belt Action Collection” bundles together some of the most iconic beat ’em up classics, including Final Fight and Captain Commando.

Each game in the collection has its charm, but I’ve got a soft spot for Final Fight. The ability to play these classics on the Switch is a dream come true.

Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles: The Cowabunga Collection (2022)

Cowabunga, dudes! The “TMNT The Cowabanga Collection” brings together the Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles’ greatest beat ’em up adventures.

While all the games are rad, “Turtles in Time” and “The Hyperstone Heist” are definite highlights. The nostalgia factor here is off the charts.

River City Girls 1 & 2 (2023)

If you’re looking for something with a more modern twist, “River City Girls 1 & 2” offers a fresh take on the beat ’em up formula. The sequel introduces new characters and locales.

Character progression is a big focus here, with skill trees and upgrades. The level design is clever, keeping the action engaging.

The anime-inspired visuals and killer soundtrack give this game a unique personality. Whether you prefer the first or second installment, both offer tons of fun.

Streets of Rage 4 / Bare Knuckle 4 (2020)

When it comes to modern beat ’em ups, “Streets of Rage 4” is an absolute must-play. It’s a testament to how the genre has evolved over the years.

The game offers a wide range of characters, each with their style. The gameplay is as deep as the streets are mean.

Visually, it’s stunning, and the soundtrack is a true banger. “Streets of Rage 4” has rejuvenated the beat ’em up genre, and it’s here to stay.

Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles: Shredder’s Revenge (2022)

“TMNT Shredders Revenge” is a love letter to TMNT fans worldwide. The nostalgia factor is off the charts.

The gameplay is straightforward and accessible, making it perfect for casual gamers and seasoned beat ’em up veterans alike.

Gather your friends, because this game is a blast in multiplayer. Plus, there’s exciting DLC content on the horizon.

The pixel art captures the essence of the classic TMNT cartoons, and the soundtrack is pure ’80s nostalgia.

Conclusion

There you have it, a rundown of some of the BEST beat ’em ups on the Nintendo Switch!

Whether you’re in it for the nostalgia, the challenging gameplay, or the sheer joy of pummeling foes, there’s a beat ’em up waiting for you on the Switch.

So, grab your Joy-Cons, invite some friends over, and let the beatdowns begin! Happy gaming!
