28 Must-Play Platformer Games on Nintendo Switch (Our Ranking)

authorLucas "ShadowStrider421" Bennett

Welcome to the ultimate guide to platforming paradise on the Nintendo Switch!

As a devoted gamer and a self-proclaimed platforming enthusiast, I’ve journeyed through the quirky, the challenging, and the downright magical worlds that these games offer.

I’m here to share my personal experiences with you, ranking 28 must-play platformers that have turned my living room into a cascade of diverse virtual adventures.

Let’s dive in!

28. INSIDE (2018)

“INSIDE” immerses me in a dark, enigmatic world of puzzles and eerie atmospheres. It feels like being part of a chilling narrative, where every silent step carries a hidden story.

The stark, minimalist art style and hauntingly beautiful sound design create an experience that’s both unsettling and captivating.

Each puzzle is a clever blend of mechanics and storytelling, making me feel like a genius and a conspirator in this dark world.

27. Super Mario Maker 2 (2019)

“Super Mario Maker 2” is a dream come true for my inner game designer. The sheer excitement of creating my own Mario levels and sharing them with friends is unparalleled.

Experimenting with classic Mario elements and the new additions feels like having an endless box of toys.

Playing levels created by the global community is a rollercoaster of creativity, from laugh-out-loud funny to fiendishly difficult. This game stands as a testament to the enduring charm and versatility of the Mario universe.

26. Disney Illusion Island (2023)

“Disney Illusion Island” offers a nostalgic trip down memory lane with a modern twist.

Teaming up with beloved characters like Mickey and Minnie, each level is a delightful journey through vibrantly animated worlds.

The game balances humor, heart, and challenge in a way that appeals to both young gamers and those young at heart.

It’s like stepping into a classic Disney cartoon, but with the interactive excitement of a modern platformer.

25. Mega Man 11 (2018)

“Mega Man 11” is a high-octane return to the past with a fresh coat of paint.

The game stays true to its roots with challenging gameplay and iconic boss battles, but the new Double Gear system adds a strategic twist that feels both innovative and respectful to the series’ legacy.

The vibrant graphics and dynamic level design make each stage a visual treat and a test of skills.

It’s a reminder of why Mega Man is such a beloved character.

24. New Super Mario Bros. U Deluxe (2019)

“New Super Mario Bros. U Deluxe” brings back the classic side-scrolling Mario experience with a polished, modern look.

It’s like revisiting an old friend who has learned some new tricks. The addition of new characters and the challenge mode adds layers of fun and replayability.

The game perfectly blends nostalgia and newness, making it a family favorite.

23. Yooka-Laylee and the Impossible Lair (2019)

“Yooka-Laylee and the Impossible Lair” is a delightful surprise, blending old-school platforming with a bright, whimsical style.

The dynamic duo of Yooka and Laylee brings a breath of fresh air to the genre.

The game cleverly mixes 2D and 3D elements, offering a variety of gameplay experiences. The humor, colorful graphics, and engaging level design keep me coming back for more.

It’s a testament to how classic platforming elements can be reimagined in creative and fun ways.

22. Cave Story+ (2017)

“Cave Story+” is a gem of an indie game that captures my imagination every time I play. Its retro-style graphics and compelling storyline make it more than just a platformer; it’s an adventure.

The game’s difficulty ramps up in such a satisfying way, and the characters I meet along the way are both quirky and endearing.

Exploring the vast, mysterious cave feels like uncovering a hidden treasure, with each area revealing more of the intriguing story.

21. Shovel Knight: Treasure Trove (2017)

“Shovel Knight: Treasure Trove” is a love letter to the retro games of yore. Every moment playing this game feels like a nostalgic trip, yet it’s packed with modern sensibilities.

The pixel art is gorgeous, and the chiptune soundtrack is catchy and evocative. Each campaign offers a unique twist on the core gameplay, keeping things fresh and exciting.

Playing as Shovel Knight, with his trusty shovel blade, offers a delightful mix of combat and platforming that never gets old.

20. LIMBO (2018)

“LIMBO” is a hauntingly beautiful game that envelops me in its eerie, monochrome world. The game’s minimalistic approach to storytelling, relying on the environment and visuals, is profoundly effective.

The puzzles are ingenious, often requiring a combination of timing and intellect.

The sense of mystery and dread is palpable as I navigate through the shadowy landscapes, making every achievement feel like a hard-won victory against the unknown.

19. Celeste (2018)

“Celeste” is not just a game; it’s an emotional journey. Playing as Madeline, battling her way up the titular mountain, is both a physical and mental challenge.

The game’s pixel art is charming, and the soundtrack is simply mesmerizing. Each level tests my reflexes and resolve, with a satisfying difficulty curve.

The story, dealing with themes of mental health and perseverance, resonates deeply with me, making every summit reached a moment of personal triumph.

18. Mega Man X Legacy Collection (2018)

“Mega Man X Legacy Collection” brings back the adrenaline rush of the original series.

Playing through these classic titles, I’m reminded of the fast-paced action and precise platforming that defined my early gaming days.

The updated graphics give the games a fresh look, while the core gameplay remains as engaging as ever.

It’s a blast from the past that still holds up today, proving the timelessness of the Mega Man X series.

17. Snake Pass (2017)

“Snake Pass” is a unique take on the platformer genre, where I slither and coil as Noodle the snake. The game’s physics-based movement is a fun and refreshing challenge.

The vibrant levels are filled with puzzles that require a different approach, making me think like a snake.

The colorful world and charming characters add to the game’s appeal, creating a relaxing yet engaging experience that stands out in the platforming genre.

16. Sonic Mania (2017)

“Sonic Mania” is a thrilling rush of nostalgia and newness. Playing this game feels like revisiting the classic Sonic experience, but with added layers of polish and creativity.

The fast-paced gameplay, iconic loops, and rings, combined with gorgeous pixel art and a fantastic soundtrack, make each level a joy to zoom through.

The game cleverly mixes old and new elements, appealing to both long-time Sonic fans and newcomers alike.

15. The Messenger (2018)

“The Messenger” starts as a classic 8-bit platformer but evolves into something much more. The game surprises me with its shift to a 16-bit style, adding a whole new dimension to the gameplay.

The witty dialogue and engaging story keep me hooked, while the time-travel mechanic adds depth to the puzzles and exploration.

It’s a game that constantly keeps me on my toes, offering a perfect blend of nostalgia and innovation.

14. GRIS (2018)

“GRIS” is an artistic masterpiece that mesmerizes me with its stunning visuals and soul-stirring soundtrack.

This game is more than just a platformer; it’s a moving piece of art. Each level represents different stages of grief, conveyed through beautiful landscapes and color changes. The platforming is intertwined with puzzle elements, creating a seamless flow that’s both calming and captivating.

Playing “GRIS” feels like a journey through a deeply personal story, one that’s poignant and uplifting in equal measure.

13. Super Mario 3D All-Stars (2020)

“Super Mario 3D All-Stars” is a nostalgic treasure trove that brings back three iconic Mario games. Each game, from “Super Mario 64” to “Super Mario Galaxy,” offers a distinct 3D platforming experience.

Revisiting these games with improved graphics and controls is a joy. It’s like walking down memory lane, but with the added excitement of rediscovering each world’s unique charm and challenges.

This collection is a must-have for Mario fans and a delightful introduction for newcomers to the series.

12. Rayman Legends (2017)

“Rayman Legends” is a vibrant and whimsical adventure that never fails to entertain me.

The game’s hand-drawn art style is absolutely gorgeous, and the levels are creatively designed with a great sense of rhythm and flow.

Playing through the musical levels is a standout experience, perfectly syncing platforming action with catchy tunes.

The cooperative gameplay adds another layer of fun, making it a great game to play with friends and family.

11. Hollow Knight (2018)

“Hollow Knight” immerses me in its hauntingly beautiful and expansive world. This game is a compelling blend of platforming, exploration, and combat.

The hand-drawn gothic art style creates an atmospheric experience, and the lore of Hallownest is deep and intriguing.

The challenge of navigating through the game’s interconnected world and battling its varied enemies is both rewarding and exhilarating.

“Hollow Knight” is a masterpiece of indie gaming that continues to captivate me.

10. Crash Bandicoot N. Sane Trilogy (2018)

The “Crash Bandicoot N. Sane Trilogy” brings back the classic Crash games with a modern facelift. The remastered graphics make the wacky world of Crash more vibrant than ever.

The platforming is as challenging and fun as I remember, with the added bonus of improved controls.

Reliving Crash’s adventures, from dodging TNT crates to riding on a polar bear, brings a sense of nostalgia mixed with fresh excitement.

9. Crash Bandicoot 4: It’s About Time (2021)

“Crash Bandicoot 4: It’s About Time” is a fantastic continuation of the beloved series. The game introduces new gameplay mechanics, like the Quantum Masks, adding fresh twists to the classic platforming formula.

The levels are brilliantly designed, filled with inventive challenges and hidden collectibles.

Playing as different characters, each with their own unique playstyle, keeps the experience fresh and engaging. It’s a worthy sequel that lives up to the Crash Bandicoot legacy.

8. Cuphead (2019)

“Cuphead” is a thrilling throwback to 1930s animation, combined with exhilarating run-and-gun action.

The game’s hand-drawn art style is simply breathtaking, and the jazz soundtrack is perfectly in tune with the aesthetic.

The boss battles are the heart of the game, each one a unique and challenging encounter.

The difficulty is tough but fair, making every victory feel like a major accomplishment. “Cuphead” is a testament to the power of creativity and perseverance in game design.

7. Ori and the Will of the Wisps (2020)

“Ori and the Will of the Wisps” is an enchanting experience that captivates me with its stunning visuals and emotional storytelling.

The fluid platforming and combat are a joy to control, and the world of Niwen is both gorgeous and haunting.

The game expands on its predecessor with new abilities and a more expansive world to explore. Each area I uncover is filled with secrets and challenges, making the journey through this game a magical experience.

6. Kirby and the Forgotten Land (2022)

“Kirby and the Forgotten Land” is a delightful and imaginative adventure that brings Kirby’s world to life in 3D.

The game’s vibrant and colorful world is a joy to explore, filled with creative levels and charming characters. Kirby’s new ability to transform into various objects adds a fresh twist to the gameplay, making each level a new experience.

The mix of exploration, puzzle-solving, and classic Kirby combat is perfectly balanced, ensuring that every moment is filled with fun and whimsy.

5. Super Mario 3D World + Bowser’s Fury (2021)

“Super Mario 3D World + Bowser’s Fury” is a fantastic package that combines classic Mario platforming with innovative new gameplay.

In “Super Mario 3D World,” the multiplayer aspect is a blast, allowing me and my friends to team up in this beautifully designed world. “Bowser’s Fury” is a delightful addition, offering an open-world experience that feels fresh and exciting.

The game’s vibrant graphics, joyful soundtrack, and varied level designs make it a standout title in the Mario series.

4. Donkey Kong Country: Tropical Freeze (2018)

“Donkey Kong Country: Tropical Freeze” is an exhilarating adventure that brings Donkey Kong to the Nintendo Switch with style.

The game’s level design is exceptional, offering a perfect blend of challenge and fun. The dynamic environments, from lush jungles to frozen landscapes, are stunning, and the soundtrack is one of the best in any platformer.

Playing as different Kong family members, each with unique abilities, adds variety to the gameplay and keeps things interesting.

3. Metroid Dread (2021)

“Metroid Dread” revives the classic Metroid formula with a modern twist. The game combines exploration, puzzle-solving, and action-packed combat in a seamless way.

Exploring the alien world is both eerie and exhilarating, with a sense of danger lurking around every corner. The addition of stealth elements and intense boss battles makes this game a thrilling experience.

The story is engaging, and the graphics are some of the best on the Nintendo Switch, making “Metroid Dread” a must-play title.

2. Super Mario Odyssey (2017)

“Super Mario Odyssey” is a masterpiece of platforming innovation. Traveling across various kingdoms with Mario and his new companion, Cappy, offers an endless array of discovery and excitement.

The game’s capture mechanic, where Mario can take control of different characters and objects, is genius, adding depth to the gameplay.

The worlds are vast and filled with secrets, making exploration a constant delight. The visuals are stunning, and the soundtrack is wonderfully whimsical, encapsulating the magic of the Mario series.

1. Super Mario Bros. Wonder (2023)

“Super Mario Bros. Wonder” is the pinnacle of platforming joy on the Nintendo Switch. This game takes everything I love about the Mario series and elevates it to new heights.

The level design is ingenious, offering a perfect mix of challenge, creativity, and fun. The controls are tight and responsive, making each jump and power-up feel rewarding.

The game’s ability to constantly surprise and delight me with new ideas and worlds is unmatched.

It’s a celebration of Mario’s legacy and a shining example of what makes platformers so enjoyable.
