The Top 25 Best Metal Slug and Contra Clone Games Ranked

authorElliot "PixelProwler" Parker

Welcome to the thrilling world of run-and-gun games, where action-packed adventures and pixel-perfect precision reign supreme!

Today, we’re diving deep into the exhilarating realm of “Metal Slug” and “Contra” clones - those magnificent titles that capture the essence of these classics while adding their unique twists.

From retro throwbacks to modern masterpieces, we’ve ranked the top 25 must-play games that will keep your trigger fingers twitchy and your heart racing.

Grab your controller, and let’s embark on this bullet-riddled journey through gaming nostalgia and innovation!

25Bay Route (1989)

Platform: Arcade

Bay Route takes you back to the roots of arcade gaming. The game, with its simple yet captivating graphics, reminds me of the countless quarters I spent in dimly lit arcades.

Its straightforward run-and-gun mechanics are a pure nostalgic bliss.

Although it doesn’t have the depth of modern titles, the charm of its pixelated enemies and the satisfaction of dodging a screen full of bullets are timeless.

Each level feels like a trip down memory lane, yet challenging enough to keep you engaged.

24Outrage (2020)

Platform: Commodore 64

Outrage is a testament to the undying appeal of the Commodore 64. Booting this game up, I was instantly transported back to the 80s, despite its 2020 release.

Its pixel art, chiptune soundtrack, and the simplicity of the gameplay are a love letter to the era.

It’s mind-blowing how this game pushes the C64 to its limits, offering a surprisingly smooth experience that rivals its arcade counterparts. Playing Outrage is like opening a time capsule - it’s a piece of history that still holds up today.

23Cyber-Lip (1990)

Platforms: Neo-Geo MVS, Neo Geo AES, Neo Geo CD; (2018 re-release) - Xbox One, Nintendo Switch, PlayStation 4

Cyber-Lip feels like it was ahead of its time when it first hit the Neo-Geo.

Its cyberpunk aesthetic and branching level paths added a fresh layer to the genre. Revisiting it on modern consoles, I appreciate how well it has aged.

The action is relentless, and the boss battles are as epic as ever. Cyber-Lip might not have the fame of its inspirations, but it certainly deserves a spot in any run-and-gun aficionado’s library.

22Soda Crisis (2022)

Platform: Microsoft Windows

Soda Crisis is a modern twist on the classic formula. Its fluid animations and fast-paced gameplay are a stark contrast to the more rigid movements of its predecessors.

This game feels like a breath of fresh air in the genre.

Its unique art style and intriguing storyline add depth to the usual run-and-gun experience. Soda Crisis proves that there’s still room for innovation in this genre.

21Vengeful Guardian: Moonrider (2023)

Platforms: PlayStation 5, PlayStation 4, Nintendo Switch, PC (Steam), Luna

Vengeful Guardian: Moonrider combines the old-school charm with modern design sensibilities. It’s like playing an 80s anime in the best way possible.

The graphics are beautifully retro, and the controls are tight and responsive. Each level introduces new challenges and enemies, keeping the gameplay fresh.

It’s proof of how far the genre has come while staying true to its roots.

20Dolphin Blue (2003)

Platform: Arcade

Dolphin Blue is an underwater adventure that’s as quirky as it is fun.

Riding dolphins and battling sea creatures, this game takes the run-and-gun formula to new, aquatic heights. The vibrant colors and smooth animations are a feast for the eyes.

It’s a unique take on the genre that shouldn’t be missed.

19Clash Force (2017)

Platforms: PC, Nintendo Switch, Xbox One, PS4, iOS

Clash Force is a love letter to the 8-bit era. The game’s pixel art and chiptune soundtrack are charmingly retro.

Its simplicity is its strength, offering straightforward, fun gameplay that’s easy to pick up but hard to master.

It’s a reminder of the days when games were all about pure, unadulterated fun.

18Iron Meat (2020)

Platform: PC

Iron Meat is a gritty, intense experience. Its dark, post-apocalyptic world is a far cry from the more colorful titles on this list.

The game is challenging, requiring precise movements and quick reflexes.

It’s a brutal yet satisfying game that will test the skills of even the most seasoned players.

17Savage Halloween (2021)

Platforms: PC, Nintendo Switch, PlayStation 4, Xbox One

Savage Halloween is a delightful blend of horror and humor. Set in a world where every day is Halloween, the game’s quirky characters and environments are a blast.

It’s not just about the shooting; the platforming elements add an extra layer of challenge.

It’s a game that doesn’t take itself too seriously, and that’s a big part of its charm.

16Rogue Stormers (2015)

Platforms: PC, PlayStation 4, Xbox One

Rogue Stormers combines run-and-gun gameplay with rogue-like elements, creating a unique and addictive experience.

Each run is different, thanks to procedurally generated levels and a variety of characters and weapons.

The game is tough but fair, and the thrill of making it just a bit further each time is incredibly rewarding.

15Oniken (2012)

Platforms: Nintendo Switch, PlayStation 4, Xbox One, Linux, Microsoft Windows, macOS, Mac OS

Oniken is a throwback to the 8-bit era in every sense. Its graphics, sound, and gameplay feel like they were ripped straight out of the late 80s.

The game is unapologetically hard, just like the classics it emulates.

It’s a game that demands precision and patience, but the sense of accomplishment when you finally beat a tough level is unmatched.

14Aliens: Infestation (2011)

Platform: Nintendo DS

Aliens: Infestation is a hidden gem on the Nintendo DS. It masterfully blends the run-and-gun style with survival horror elements.

The game captures the tense atmosphere of the “Aliens” movies, with tight corridors and deadly Xenomorphs lurking around every corner.

It’s a must-play for fans of the franchise and the genre alike.

13Zombotron (2019)

Platform: PC

Zombotron feels like an action movie in video game form.

The game’s physics-based combat adds a layer of strategy to the usual run-and-gun formula.

Blasting zombies and watching the environment react in real-time is immensely satisfying.

It’s a game that encourages creativity in how you approach combat.

12RIVE: Wreck, Hack, Die, Retry (2016)

Platforms: Windows, Linux, Macintosh, PlayStation 4

RIVE is a frantic and fun experience.

The game’s twin-stick shooter mechanics are a nice change of pace, and the ability to hack enemies adds a tactical element.

Its fast-paced gameplay and witty dialogue keep things entertaining from start to finish.

RIVE is a game that doesn’t let up, and that’s a good thing.

11Super Cyborg (2014)

Platforms: Nintendo Switch, PlayStation 4, Xbox One, Microsoft Windows, Xbox Series X e Series S

Super Cyborg is a love letter to Contra.

The game captures the essence of its inspiration, from the challenging gameplay to the iconic weapon power-ups.

It’s like stepping into a time machine and playing a long-lost 90s classic.

For fans of the genre, this game is a must-play.

10Warhammer 40,000: Shootas, Blood & Teef (2022)

Platforms: Microsoft Windows, PlayStation 5, Xbox Series X/S, Mac, Nintendo Switch, PlayStation 4, Xbox One

Warhammer 40,000: Shootas, Blood & Teef is an absolute blast.

Its over-the-top action and dark humor perfectly capture the spirit of the Warhammer universe.

The game’s hand-drawn art style is a visual treat, and the cooperative gameplay makes it even more fun to play with friends.

It’s a game that combines great mechanics with a great setting.

9Butcher (2016)

Platforms: PC, PlayStation 4, Xbox One, Nintendo Switch

Butcher is as brutal as its name suggests.

The game’s pixel art is dripping with gore, and its industrial soundtrack sets the perfect tone.

It’s a challenging game that rewards aggressive play.

Butcher is not for the faint of heart, but if you’re looking for a hardcore run-and-gun experience, it’s hard to beat.

8Mighty Goose (2021)

Platforms: Windows, macOS, Linux, PlayStation 4, PlayStation 5, Nintendo Switch, Xbox One, Xbox Series X/S

Mighty Goose is run-and-gun silliness at its best. Playing as a bounty hunting goose is as absurd and fun as it sounds.

The game’s cartoonish graphics and over-the-top weapons add to the overall enjoyment.

It’s a game that doesn’t take itself seriously and is all the better for it.

7Nex Machina (2017)

Platforms: PlayStation 4, Windows-based personal computers

Nex Machina is a visual and auditory feast.

The game’s neon-soaked levels and thumping electronic soundtrack are a treat.

Its twin-stick shooting mechanics are smooth and responsive, making for an exhilarating experience.

Nex Machina is a modern classic that shows the enduring appeal of the genre.

6Super Time Force Ultra (2014)

Platforms: Xbox platforms, Microsoft Windows, PlayStation 4, PlayStation Vita

Super Time Force Ultra is a unique twist on the genre.

Its time-travel mechanics allow you to rewind and play alongside past versions of yourself, creating a strategic layer to the usual run-and-gun gameplay.

The game’s humor and retro-inspired graphics are the icing on the cake.

It’s a clever and fun title that stands out from the crowd.

5Midnight Resistance (1989)

Platforms: Mega Drive, Commodore 64, Arcade, Amiga, ZX Spectrum, Atari ST, AmigaOS, Amstrad CPC

Midnight Resistance is a classic that still holds up today.

Its weapon upgrade system was innovative for its time, and the co-op gameplay adds to the fun.

The game’s soundtrack is catchy, and its levels are well-designed.

It’s a piece of gaming history that’s still enjoyable to play.

4Ghosts ‘N Goblins Resurrection (2021)

Platforms: Nintendo Switch, PlayStation 4, Xbox One, Microsoft Windows

Ghosts ‘N Goblins Resurrection is a beautifully crafted reboot of a classic.

The game retains the punishing difficulty of the original while updating the graphics and controls for a modern audience.

It’s a game that will test your skills and patience, but overcoming its challenges is incredibly rewarding.

3Valfaris (2019)

Platforms: Nintendo Switch, PlayStation 4, Xbox One, Microsoft Windows, Amazon Luna, Mac OS

Valfaris is a metal masterpiece. The game’s heavy metal soundtrack and dark, sci-fi aesthetic are a perfect match.

Its gameplay is tight and satisfying, with a variety of weapons and enemies to keep things interesting.

Valfaris is a headbanger’s dream come true.

2Huntdown (2020)

Platforms: Microsoft Windows, macOS, PlayStation 4, Xbox One, Nintendo Switch, Android, iOS

Huntdown is an action-packed thrill ride. The game’s pixel art and voice acting are top-notch, creating a rich and immersive world.

Its humor and references to action movies of the 80s and 90s add to the charm.

Huntdown is a game that manages to be both a tribute and a standout title in its own right.

1Axiom Verge (2015)

Platforms: PlayStation 4, Linux, OS X, Windows, PlayStation Vita, Wii U, Xbox One

Axiom Verge stands at the pinnacle of this list.

This game is more than just a homage to Metroid; it’s an evolution of the genre. Its sprawling world, filled with secrets and mysteries, is a joy to explore.

The game’s retro graphics and soundtrack are beautifully done, and its story is intriguing and well-told.

Axiom Verge isn’t just a great run-and-gun game; it’s a great game, period.

There you have it, the top 25 Metal Slug and Contra clone games that are sure to delight fans of the genre. Each title brings something unique to the table, whether it’s a twist on the classic formula, a modern take on retro graphics, or just sheer, unadulterated fun. Happy gaming!
